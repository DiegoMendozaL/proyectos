Django
Reinhardt

The most astonishing guitar player ever.

Jean "Django" Reinhardt was a Belgian-born French jazz guitarist and composer of Romani ethnicity, regarded as one of the greatest musicians of the twentieth century, having written nearly 100 songs.

He was the first and most significant jazz talent to emerge from Europe.

The 3 fingered-guitar player

Despite having two of his fingers disabled from a fire, he overcame the handicap and went on to forge an entirely new style of jazz guitar technique (sometimes called 'hot' jazz guitar), which has since become a living musical tradition within French Gypsy culture.

Benny Goodman asked him to travel with his band, which he never did, but he did tour the U.S. with Duke Ellington's band in 1946. He died suddenly of a stroke at age 43.

Many guitar players and other musicians have expressed admiration for Reinhardt or have cited him as a major influence. Jeff Beck described Reinhardt as "by far the most astonishing guitar player ever" and "quite superhuman".

World War II

Because Reinhardt and his family were Gypsies, and he was also a jazz musician, he tried to escape from occupied France with his family. During the occupation of France, Reinhardt continued playing and composing. One of his songs, "Nuages," became an unofficial anthem in Paris to signify hope for liberation. One day at a concert, the popularity of the song was such that the crowd made him replay the song three times in a row.
